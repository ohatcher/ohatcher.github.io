The Surface of a Leaf

Surface of a Leaf

2022
clay, paint, seed beads, fiber
5x7"

This project explores the element earth, specifically the gingko leaf and the properties of its surface. The sculpture was inspired by an image captured by a scanning electron microscope of the surface of the gingko leaf at x284 magnification. The sculpture starts out as a 3D replica of that magnified image, and then morphs into an imagined version of what it might look like to keep zooming in closer and closer to the surface of that leaf. Because the image from my research resembled hills, I used that as a starting point when building the rest of the sculpture, and it became a visualization of what worlds and landscapes might exist on the small surface of a leaf. In creating this sculpture, the aim was to explore the possibilities of what could exist at such a small scale, as well as to bring a sense of whimsy to something as common as a leaf.
